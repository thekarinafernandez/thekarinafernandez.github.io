Builder.

I didn't come to tech through a classroom. I came through a problem I couldn't stop thinking about — and a decade of running operations that made me realize most tools weren't built by people who actually use them.

I'm a single mom based in Tacoma, WA. I build around Jude's schedule, ship things late at night, and document everything so nothing gets lost. Being a parent in tech isn't a liability — it's made me faster at prioritizing, sharper at problem-solving, and completely intolerant of wasted time.

I've been teaching myself the fundamentals since 2022 — working through concepts, studying how products are built, learning the stack piece by piece. In April 2026 I started shipping: Relay, a full AI operations product, and Sigil System, a persistent cross-platform AI companion with a live backend, mobile app, and working memory architecture. Both built solo, end to end.

I'm drawn to the intersection of AI and product design — how intelligence gets shaped into something a human can actually feel. Sigil is the most honest work I've made.

I'm actively seeking a full-time remote role at a larger company where I can contribute real operational and technical value while continuing to grow as a developer. I want roots somewhere — a team, a codebase, a product I can invest in long-term — while I keep building my skills on the side.

MVP structure — monorepo, backend, Telegram bot, mobile shell

Created the full monorepo structure. Built a Node.js backend API, a Telegram bot connector, and an Expo mobile app shell — all connected. First time understanding how frontend and backend communicate. Learned how local IP addresses let a phone reach a local server. Voice phase — permissions, state tracking, call tab shell

Added the first real Call tab shell to the mobile app. Implemented microphone permission handling and basic voice state tracking.
